George Sr. mourned his son's death in giving money away.
The son maybe dead, but his memory would be kept alive.
The now childless, bereft man didn't need to leave anything behind. The memory of his son would become his legacy.

He gave money to universities.
One where his son actually went to. In Harvard they built the freshmen residency in his name.
The other half was given to Northwestern.
They named the entire campus that is now known as the Chicago Campus after the dead son.

George Sr. lost all his money in the great depression. Or at least he lost enough in order to ask for the donation back.
He asked only from Northwestern. Or at least only there he actually got anything back. Harvard didn't mention any request.

When the university returned the money, they also returned his name.
He gave the money in order to keep the memory alive. Now both the memory is gone from there and the gates are dead.
