A gate usually has the ability to open, but even without the ability to close it has the importance of delineating an outside and an inside. This gates can no longer open, so they are closer to a wall, marking where an opening used to be. But even more diminutive is the fact that they are no longer attached to anything. Floating in the middle of the road, no outside or inside can be discerned between the sides of the gate. The gates are ornate and now can be seen as a memorial for the craft of the master blacksmith that made them. Metal flowers and floral patterns cover every inch of the gates. Snake shaped door handles mark where you would open it. I rather see the snake as a symbol of wisdom and knowledge. There is something poetic about the symbol of knowledge has to be held in order to open the gates to knowledge. You need to know something in order to open the gates for knowing.
Those gates are permanently closed so that is not really happening. The gates are a beautiful shell of a gate. A reminder not of whatever they were built for, but of a time of ornate memorials.
